I am an interdisciplinary researcher and performance producer from Shanghai, China. I critically examine how technology performs and how people perform technology through both live performance production and computational system prototypes.

My research primarily lies at the intersection of Performance Studies and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), viewing interaction as a performative and dramaturgical process that structures collaboration between human and computational agents.

My artistic practice centers on understanding interaction both within and beyond performance systems. I approach management as a dramaturgy of performance, a creative logic that composes relations among arts, technology, and society, revealing how systems themselves perform.

I received my B.A. in Arts Management from Shanghai Theatre Academy and a Bachelor-Minor Certificate in Science and Engineering (Computer Science & Food Science) from Shanghai Jiao Tong University in June 2024. I am currently pursuing a second B.S. in Accounting at Beijing Normal University to extend my dramaturgical thinking into the quantitative and systemic dimensions of production.
